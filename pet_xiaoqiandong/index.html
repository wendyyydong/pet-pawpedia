2024 Animal Shelter Statistics

In 2024, approximately 5.8 million dogs and cats entered shelters and rescues. While 4.2 million animals were adopted, shelters continued to face capacity challenges.

Shelter Progress

Euthanasia rates in shelters have continued to decline over the past five years.

In 2019, approximately 13% of shelter animals were euthanized. By 2024, that number had decreased to around 8%.

Although shelters nationwide still face overcrowding and capacity challenges, increased adoption efforts and rescue support have helped improve outcomes for many animals.

After Adoption, What Comes Next?

Bringing a dog home is only the beginning of the journey. Beyond providing food and shelter, dogs rely on daily care, exercise, hydration, medical attention, and emotional companionship to live healthy and happy lives.

Understanding their basic needs can help owners create a safer, more comfortable environment and build a stronger lifelong bond with their pets.

Hydration Tips

Fresh Water

Bring extra water when traveling or spending time outdoors.

⚠️
Watch Signs

Dehydration

Signs may include lethargy, dry gums, decreased appetite, or unusual tiredness.

Contact a vet if symptoms seem serious or persistent.

💧 These are general guidelines. Individual needs may vary based on age, activity level, diet, climate, and overall health. Always ensure fresh water is available.

🍽️

Regular Feeding

Feed your dog on a consistent daily schedule with balanced nutrition and appropriate portion sizes based on their age, size, and activity level. Most adult dogs are typically fed 1-2 times per day, while puppies may require smaller and more frequent meals to support healthy growth and energy.

🎾

Daily Exercise

Most dogs benefit from at least 30-60 minutes of daily exercise, depending on their breed, age, and energy level. Daily walks, playtime, and outdoor activities help keep dogs physically healthy, mentally stimulated, and emotionally happy.

🩺

Vet Checkups

Routine veterinary visits can help identify health concerns early and support long-term wellness. Regular checkups, vaccinations, dental care, and parasite prevention all play an important role in keeping your dog healthy throughout different stages of life.

✂️

Grooming Care

Regular brushing, bathing, nail trimming, and coat care help keep your dog clean, comfortable, and healthy. Grooming also helps reduce shedding, prevents matting, and allows owners to notice potential skin issues or abnormalities early.

Common Toxic Plants for Dogs

⚠️ Important: If you suspect your dog has ingested any toxic plant, seek immediate veterinary care.

Many common household and garden plants can be harmful to dogs, ranging from mild stomach upset to severe or life-threatening poisoning. Some of the most dangerous examples include Sago Palm, Oleander, Azaleas, Lilies, and Castor Oil Plant.

Houseplants

Aloe vera, Snake plant, Pothos, Jade plant, Peace lilies, and Dracaena.

Garden Plants

Oleander, Azaleas/Rhododendrons, Sago palm, Foxglove, Hydrangeas, and Yew.

Flowers & Bulbs

Tulips, Hyacinths, Daffodils, and Lily of the Valley.

Other Plants

English Ivy, Rhubarb, Onion, Garlic, and Hemlock.

Toxic Plant Symptoms in Dogs

Common signs to watch for if your dog may have eaten a toxic plant.

🤢

Vomiting or diarrhea

Upset stomach is one of the most common early signs.

💧

Excessive drooling

Dogs may drool more than usual after chewing toxic leaves or stems.

😞

Lethargy or weakness

Your dog may seem unusually tired, quiet, or less active.

🌬️

Difficulty breathing

Breathing changes may signal a more serious reaction.

❤️

Abnormal heart rate

Some toxic plants can affect the heart and circulation.

⚠️

Tremors or seizures

Seek emergency veterinary care right away if these appear.

📞

If you suspect your dog has eaten a toxic plant, call your veterinarian immediately or the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at (888) 426-4435.

What Colors Can Dogs See

Dogs can generally detect black and white in terms of light and dark contrast. They can also detect yellow and blue, including different shades of those colors.

Colors Dogs See Differently

Dogs cannot distinguish red and green the same way humans do because they only have two types of cones. Their color perception is similar to red-green color vision deficiency in humans, while shades of yellow and blue appear much more vibrant to them.

Through Your Dog's Eyes

Dogs see the world with less color variety than humans, but they are still very good at detecting motion and contrast.

We hope every dog can grow up surrounded by love, comfort, and companionship — and that every owner can cherish the beautiful memories they create together throughout their journey.
